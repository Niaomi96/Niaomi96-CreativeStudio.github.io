Prologue Part 1

// Where the clouds kisses the land upon the mountains, there is a place not known to man, a lush eden within the cusp of a mountain, where fauna is drastically different from their counterparts below. Where a wolf species dwells not like wolves but the big cats of the great African plains.. \\


Wind gusts swirl and weave through the sweet autumn trees making leaves fall and the branches barren, and unfruitful. The wind blows through the she wolf fur, she sighs in relief as she looks over to their pack male that produces with all females many females having young of their own. She grooms her overgrown belly, she gets up and goes and greets her pack protector, she runs her head on his then leaves the pack to find a safe place to deliver her precious unborn pups. This place may be peaceful and safe, however it's far from it. Other males will give nothing to take over her pack and slaughter their young. The she wolf climbs a tree into a den, as she has been making a nest for quite some time in the tree hollow. She enters and rolls around and prepares herself for a long day, as she pants heavily, she feels secluded and safe in her nest


// Typically, females will have up to one to three pups per litter.\\


Few hours later a single pup was born into this world, she was covered in black and brown coat. She nurses on her mother as the only pup she will get the best start in life. The mother grooms her bundle of joy, however, was highly alert and aware of her surroundings. The little pup will have to earn her name in a right of passage. The pup purrs as it is now asleep and wrapped around by her mother. Two weeks have passed since the little female was born and her eyes are starting to open. Everything seems blurry and the light glistening on the side of her eyes wasn't very helpful to her. She rubs her eyes and whimpers she was alone in the den at this time, she gives off a cute puppy yawn/ whine.

Her mother came and asked,'' What are you up to little one?" The little she wolf looks up at her mother with the most inspiration and states," watching the animals that walk on water," she giggles. The little female emerges from the tall autumn grass she then runs around popping in and out of the grass. What if I don't make it to her in time." She muttered under her breath. Her mother stopped at their hiding spot and looked frantically around, when she saw her pup go under the water. "Oh My Goodness!", she screamed and went into the water after her. The little female started falling to the bottom of the lake, she tried to swim her way up, but she couldn't and she was running out of time. She felt her lungs constrict, she needed to inhale, she was about to, when her mother pulled her out the water. She gasped frantically. She panicked at first, not realizing what was happening, then she looked up and saw her mother holding her and taking her to safety. Her mother placed her down, glaring at her, and she smiled with ears back. " But mama, I just wanted to swim like the ducks," she said while lowering her head, "Don't ever do that again, you could have drowned," she stated with heavy breathing. She realized her little one was very much afraid, she nearly drowned. She cups her daughter's cheek with one of her big paws, and places her head onto her daughter's head. She leads her away from the water edge. The she wolf grooms her soaked pup dry, also stimulating her to breathe more and to sooth her. Her daughter falls asleep in her arms. She had a very traumatic, but fun day.

She looks around and sees this thick haze has fallen up her surroundings, she goes to the bank of the lake and lays next to it. She was very curious about what was swimming underneath the lake. She shoves her head into the water holding her breath, she watches fish swimming by. Her tail wags, in a mischievous way, she pulls her head out and shakes her coat. She walked into a shallow part of the lake and splashed around in it. She comes out and shakes her coat once more then climbs the tree that was near where her mother always leaves her to go hunt. She makes it to split ways of the tree trunk and climbs higher. Then she lays in its branches purring as she watches the fog dispatch slowly but surely, putting her to sleep. After her nap, she climbed down, and noticed a single song bird, pulling up a worm. She started stalking it, practicing her skills she will someday need in life. She was so close, but the bird spotted her and took flight, she stalked and waited too long to strike, she was also very noisy. However, the bird kept getting its worm as it saw the young predator as naive and incapable of harming it, till she got close enough to it, that's when it retreated, and retreat it did, it landed on a rock and ate its worm, right in front of the little female, then flew away. Her tummy begins to grumble, and just on time mom has returned but she had something special for her little one. The little female comes running to her mother. She stops abruptly not sure what that thing is in her mom's mouth, she looks curiously at it. She drops the boar leg in front of her daughter, she wags her tail and hopes this will encourage her to try her first taste of meat. The little female paws at it then pounces on it not sure if it's a toy or food but it smelt amazing to her. She bits it while she licks it with her back legs. It was long overdue, the little she wolf wasn't happy about the new hiding spot and mopped all the way there. They stopped next to the cliffs, and her mother started climbing up them. The little female had to keep up which was hard for her to do because she was yet so tall. She whines to her mother to pick her up instead, but she ignores her. She won't be joining the pack for another month, so it's important for her to learn to keep up or perish as they aren't the only predators here in these mountains.
